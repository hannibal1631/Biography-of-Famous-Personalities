Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose

1897 - unknown

Life of Netaji

Subhas Chandra Bose, widely known as Netaji, emerged as a towering figure in India's fight for independence during the early to mid-20th century. Born on January 23, 1897, he rose through the ranks of the Indian National Congress but eventually parted ways due to ideological differences, forming the Forward Bloc to advocate for a more militant approach to liberation. During World War II, Bose sought support from Nazi Germany and Imperial Japan to overthrow British rule, leading to the establishment of the Indian National Army (INA). Despite initial successes, including the INA's military campaign alongside Japanese forces in Southeast Asia, the tide turned against the Axis powers, culminating in Bose's mysterious death in a plane crash in August 1945. Nevertheless, his legacy as a passionate nationalist and charismatic leader, who inspired millions to fight for freedom, remains indelible in India's history, making Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose an enduring symbol of the nation's struggle for independence.
